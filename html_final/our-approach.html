Our Approach

Our team initially wanted to explore the possible impacts on commuting times, but we found that the factors that may affect commute times were not available in our dataset, and that information would be difficult to find. We then chose to try and predict the causes of fatal injuries in car crashes, using data from the city of Chicago. Our dataset looked at crash data from 2017-2019.

Our dataset came from the City of Chicago website. Extracting this data into a CSV file, we then transformed the data using Pandas. We set up a machine learning library (scikit-learn) to train and test data pertaining to crashes, as well as the predictive factors that could cause them. Factors that we focused on included: crash hour vs. posted speed limit, road conditions vs. crash damage, crash type vs. damage, and weather condition vs. crash type. Leveraging Matplotlib for visualization of the data, we then tested our factors to see if any of these were statistically significant in correlation to car crashes with fatal injuries.

We then created a heat map of car crashes in Chicago using an API, plotting all recorded crashes within the city limits of Chicago from 2015-2019.

Additionally, we made a logistical regression machine learning model trying to determine conditions leading to crashes, and whether the injuries were fatal or not.

To present our data, we built a website to showcase our work.
